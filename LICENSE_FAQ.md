# DexterJS License FAQ

DexterJS is licensed under the GNU Lesser General Public License v3 (LGPL-3.0).

## What you CAN do

- ✅ Use DexterJS in your personal or commercial app for free
- ✅ Use DexterJS in a closed-source commercial product
- ✅ Modify DexterJS for your own internal use
- ✅ Contribute back to DexterJS

## What you CANNOT do

- ❌ Build a wrapper library or competing product on top of DexterJS and keep it closed source — if you distribute a product built on DexterJS, that product must also be open source under LGPL
- ❌ Remove or modify the license headers
- ❌ Claim you wrote the original DexterJS

## In simple terms

If you're a developer using DexterJS to monitor your app — you're completely free. No restrictions.

If you're a company trying to build a paid monitoring product on top of DexterJS — your product must also be open source.
